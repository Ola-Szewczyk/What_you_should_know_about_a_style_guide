A style guide makes a difference

A style guide can improve the quality of the documentation and therefore the brand communication, without a doubt. For the purpose of this analysis, Microsoft Writing Style Guide serves as the point of reference. Provided summaries and examples, however, do not exhaust the extent of guidance included in the style guide, but cover selected aspects of chosen topics.

Tables

Probably every manual or user guide includes at least one table. The clarity it provides and the ability to synthesize substantial information make it omnipresent in a variety of deliverables. So, what does a perfect table look like?

The Microsoft Writing Style Guide suggests including a title or even an introduction to make the purpose of the table clear. As for the table itself, it should always have a header, and the most important information should be presented in the column on the left.

Lists

A list is every technical writer’s best friend. Synthetic, straight to the point—it contains only substantial information. The Microsoft Writing Style Guide instructs to use no more than seven short items. The writer should also be disciplined when it comes to the structure of each item—the best list is a consistent list.

Just like a table, a list’s purpose should be clear, therefore an introduction or a title is welcome.

Example of a numbered list

  1. Installing software on your computer.
  2. Select the Download button at the bottom of the page.
  3. Go to Downloads folder.
  4. Double-click the .exe file.
  5. Follow the instructions in the dialog box.

Example of a bulleted list

Basic Git functions.

  • Tracking changes in computer files.
  • Adding content.
  • Merging content added by other team members.
  • Creating branches.
  • Reverting changes.

Numbers

Consistency is the key when it comes to numbers in the documentation. With multiple ways of describing one thing with numerals, it’s easy to confuse the readers or even mislead them—especially when you are referring to the elements of the UI.

The Microsoft Writing Style Guide provides writers with multiple examples of how to keep the numerals in the text consistent.

Acronyms

Multiple-word acronyms may be equally hard for a writer to repeat throughout the entire document, and for the user to read over and over. Wisely and consistently used, acronyms and abbreviations make the text user-friendly.

Microsoft Writing Style Guide advises spelling the term out before introducing the acronym. Whenever the acronym appears in the text it should be joined by an appropriate article or made plural by adding -s.

Example

An anti-lock braking system (ABS) is a safety feature consisting of sensors and solenoid valves that control wheel speed.

Active and passive voice

This is probably one of the first things a beginner technical writer learns about a style guide. At the same time, it’s often one of the most difficult habits to lose. Active voice is easier to read and understand, especially when a writer describes complicated topics. However, there are situations when passive voice is a good choice, for example when a writer needs to emphasize the receiver of the action.

Examples

Passive: Wind energy is turned into electricity by a wind turbine.

Active: A wind turbine turns wind energy into electricity.

Passive: Electricity is carried over long distances via transmission lines from the wind turbines to its destination.

Active: Transmission lines carry electricity over long distances from the wind turbines to its destination.

Responsive content

The times of one type of output, usually MS Word, are long gone. Most of the documents developed by technical writers have to present well on different types of devices. In that case, simplicity is the key to success. Short, concise portions of text, and simple elements, such as tables or images, look good even on small screens.

Interactions with UI

The Microsoft Writing Style Guide advises on selecting the right words that are universal enough to work with all types of input. Screenshots, videos, and infographics are among the preferred methods that help obtain the clearest instruction.
