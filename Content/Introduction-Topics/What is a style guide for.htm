What is a style guide for

There are many reasons why it’s good to have and use a style guide.

Consistency

Consistency is the key purpose for having and using a style guide. It helps the writer to keep discipline and make sure that the language used in any given document matches the style of other types of written communication.

A brand that consistently presents its style across all types of deliverables is perceived as more professional. Its customers’ awareness, when they don’t get confused over the vocabulary choices or inconsistent style of communication, is much stronger.

Time management

Editors’ work is so much easier when they have access to a style guide. After they familiarize themselves with the document, they can save a lot of time by simply referring to it instead of going through the previously developed documentation to learn the desired style.

A style guide is just as important and time-saving whenever the deliverables are localized. It also helps translators to better understand the context and convey the original meaning in other languages.

Clear message

A confused reader is a frustrated reader. Any brand that wants to be perceived as professional should make sure its customers understand the message. It’s much easier to achieve such an effect by following the rules set out in a style guide.

In some cases, misunderstandings and confusion caused by incoherent style across documentation may lead to potentially dangerous situations.

Problems with understanding and interpreting the meaning could also end in legal and financial consequences, not to mention the loss of reputation.

For example, an incorrectly constructed garden shredder user manual could potentially lead to severe injuries, which could result in a consecutive lawsuit.

Adding appropriate supplements to a style guide, such as directives, recommendations, and standards, helps to improve the final outcome.

The tone

A unique voice and a defined perception of a brand is the key to success, therefore a style guide should also indicate the preferred tone. Organizations focused on highly advanced technologies or medical areas will probably keep their tone more informative and formal, whereas creative agencies will wow their recipients with a lavish style.

Culture and respectful approach

The right choice of words is more than just making sure the customers understand the message and don’t get confused over the nomenclature. The right choice of words also means that people don’t feel offended or excluded. A good style guide serves as a reference for the writer and helps to avoid the use of insulting or alienating language.
