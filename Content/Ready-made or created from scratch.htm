Ready-made or created from scratch

There are no rules saying what a good style guide consists of. Depending on the needs of the organization, a style guide’s length, contents, and purpose may vary greatly. A one-page-style-guide may be as efficient a tool as a hundreds-of-pages-document specifying every possible detail of produced deliverables.

What to expect from the ready-made style guides

Technical writers can choose from a variety of accessible style guides, such as the ones created by Google or Microsoft. They are extensive, which makes them a valuable source of knowledge and inspiration.

Google developer documentation style guide

Created with developers in mind, this style guide’s aim is to be the point of reference for content creators. It covers a variety of topics from the right choice of words to strategies that help writers to avoid misunderstandings, that, as mentioned before, could result in legal consequences.

The work on a style guide has begun in Google in 2005, but it wasn’t until 2017 that it became a public resource. It’s a living and ever-evolving record of writing and editorial decisions made by technical writers responsible for Google’s documentation.

Its advantage is that it’s not intended just for technical writers. Google developer documentation style guide supports engineers, architects, open-source communities, and other professionals responsible for creating consistent documentation.

Microsoft Writing Style Guide

Microsoft has provided writers with guidelines since 1995. The current Microsoft Writing Style Guide is a new version of the Microsoft Manual of Style from 20212.

Its scope makes it a comprehensive source of writing instruction for content creators across the IT industry. At the same time, it’s universal enough to be used by companies outside the IT industry, that want their language to be customer-centric. This style guide is very often used as a base or point of reference for other style guides, for example, Google.

A style guide created from scratch

Creation of a style guide from scratch may turn out to be a time- and cost-consuming process. It’s worth considering the use of which elements is actually relevant and ensures efficiency.

To make sure that a newly-developed style guide consists of the elements that will later serve as the roadmap, it’s a good practice to take some time and prepare for the challenge ahead.

Do the research

The documentation team should begin by gathering as many good and bad examples of already existing documents as possible. The good examples help to establish a general style and tone. They can also indicate what a style guide should consist of. The bad examples point to the things that the writers should avoid in the future and help to choose the best solutions once compared with the good ideas.

Know your audience

Another important thing is to identify the users of a style guide. The decision to create a style guide could be considered a bold one, so doing it right is crucial. The needs of the users shape the contents of the document.

In order to avoid generalization and make sure that the content is exactly what the user needs, creation of persona(s) is an important stage of working on a style guide.

There are many ready-to-use templates that provide guidance, and they usually include the following steps.

Gather as much data about the users as possible. It’s worth asking who this person is, and what their goals as well as pain points are. At this point, a writer can shape further steps of the process of persona creation. What is more important, the answers allow to define the purpose of creating a style guide.

Having collected all the elements of the puzzle, a writer can move on to the next step–organizing data. Ordering, dividing into sections, and finding connections by using spreadsheets or affinity diagrams, give a broad view of an emerging persona.

Building a persona out of the ready elements is the last step. The outcome should present as realistic user as possible, including a name and a photo.

Think about the outputs

A wise thing to do before the development phase is to establish the outputs. The final formats depend on the abovementioned needs. The team should consider whether a PDF is enough or if there’s a need to add a static webpage. Some organizations make their style guide publicly available, which may dictate the tools that should be used to create the document.

Include examples

Gathering real-life examples is especially useful when new joiners have to quickly take over and start working on a project. The theoretical part sets the rules and guidelines, the examples show how the theory is implemented. For many users, they are a source of inspiration or simply templates that save time and provide consistency throughout the entire documentation.

An extensive preparation phase may result in a ready outline of a future style guide to be developed.

Consider hybrid approach

There is one more approach that combines the use of a ready style guide and a style guide created from scratch. In most cases, a ready document, for example, Microsoft Writing Style Guide, is more than enough to help the writer develop consistent deliverables. Therefore, the complementary document is usually short and consists only of the features specific to the organization. For some industries, adding legal documentation may also be an important part of a style guide creation.
